A supercomputer is capable of performing 2 quadrillion operations per second, equivalent to 200,000 years of human labor. But it was the human mind that made the supercomputer possible. What, then, is the human brain truly capable of?

Serghei Barhatov

The mind’s capacity to shape reality goes beyond creating machines. It influences how we perceive, interpret, and engage with the world, often in ways we barely understand. Consider the following intriguing aspects of human cognition:

  • Neuroplasticity: The brain can rewire itself in response to new experiences, continuously evolving throughout a person’s life.
  • Synesthesia: Some people can "hear" colors or "see" sounds, revealing the brain's unique ways of cross-wiring sensory experiences.
  • Cognitive biases: Our brains filter information through biases like the confirmation bias, shaping our worldview in ways we’re often unaware of.
  • Memory manipulation: Our memories are not fixed; each time we recall an event, the brain subtly alters it based on new experiences, emotions.
